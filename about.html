About Our Sisterhood

National History

alpha Kappa Delta Phi International Sorority, Inc. was established at the University of California at Berkeley in the fall of 1989, and recognized by the College Panhellenic Association on February 7, 1990. The fourteen founding sisters dedicated themselves to establishing a strong and lasting organization, which would provide Asian-American women the opportunity to participate in the Greek system.

Since then, we have grown to become one of the largest international Asian-interest sororities, with over 65 chapters across North America. We strive to maintain the highest levels of integrity and innovation in the promotion of sisterhood, philanthropy, scholastic excellence, and Asian-Awareness in the community.

Our Chapter History

In the fall of 1995, Carol Chen, Joyce Chu, Jenny Hsieh, Chetali Kuber, Trinh Le, Tina Wu, and Wendy Yang brought alpha Kappa Delta Phi to the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill to enhance Asian Awareness and strengthen the voice of Asian American women.

The chapter's pre-associate status was granted on November 26, 1995. Through the traditions of love and friendship that characterized the sorority itself, the seven women of the Charter Class became active sisters of alpha Kappa Delta Phi on March 16, 1996.

Philanthropy: Breast Cancer Awareness

Our national philanthropy is dedicated towards increasing Breast Cancer Awareness (BCA). We commit to raising funds for breast cancer research and patient support. Every October, during Breast Cancer Awareness Month, our chapter hosts various events to educate the community and support this cause close to our hearts.

Through our partnership with the American Cancer Society and other organizations, we hope to make a lasting impact in the fight against breast cancer.
